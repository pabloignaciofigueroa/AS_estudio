# Motion And Interaction

## Motion Philosophy

Use motion as infrastructure.

Do not animate everything.
Animate what establishes hierarchy, proof, or continuity.

## Core Motion Families

Reuse a small family of motion patterns:

- fade + translate reveal
- scroll-driven opacity change
- typewriter sequencing
- tab indicator travel
- slider movement with easing
- hover feedback with scale or underline

## Hero Choreography

If the project can support it, use:

- a full-height hero
- a moving or living background layer
- a concise headline
- a controlled entrance timeline

In Antigravity, the hero uses:

- fixed full-screen video
- particles
- typed headline
- sequential reveals

## Scroll Strategy

Use scroll to:

- fade the hero background
- reveal cards only once when useful
- pin or partially pin sections when the section genuinely benefits from it

Do not pin blocks just to look impressive.

## Slider Strategy

Treat sliders as product components:

- enable drag
- support next and previous controls
- pause autoplay on hover
- only autoplay in viewport
- keep easing polished
- expose active state clearly

## Video Strategy

Wrap video in a premium interaction shell:

- preview frame
- cursor or overlay cue
- modal or focused playback
- clean close action

The point is to package proof, not just embed media.

## Typed Headers

Use typed headings sparingly:

- hero
- high-focus CTA sections
- maybe one or two strategic product moments

Avoid using them in every section.

## Particle Strategy

Use particles only if they support:

- atmosphere
- perceived technical sophistication
- section identity

Make them configurable by:

- density
- scale
- displacement
- camera or parallax feel

Do not let them compete with legibility.
